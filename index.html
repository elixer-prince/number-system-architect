Foundation I

What is the Decimal system?

The Decimal system (also called Base-10) uses ten symbols: 0-9. It's the system we use every day because we have ten fingers.

Foundation II

What is the Binary system?

The Binary system (also called Base-2) uses only two symbols: 0 and 1. It is the fundamental language of computers, representing "Off" and "On" states.

The Algorithm

How do we convert?

To find the binary version of a decimal number, we use the Repeated Division Method.

1

Divide by 2

Take your number and split it in half. Binary is Base-2, so we always use 2.

2

Watch the Remainder

Is there a leftover? That remainder (0 or 1) is your actual binary digit!

3

Repeat

Keep dividing the result until you hit zero. The digits are read from bottom to top.

💡

Think of it like peeling an onion. Every time you divide by 2, you are peeling away the least significant part of the number to find out if it's even (0) or odd (1).

Moving to the Machine

You've just found the sequence: 1101. In a computer, these aren't just numbers on a page—they are physical states.

The "1" (High)

Represents an "On" switch, allowing electricity to flow. Each switch represents a specific value (1, 2, 4, 8...).

The "0" (Low)

Represents an "Off" switch. We skip that value entirely. Zero is just as important as one!
